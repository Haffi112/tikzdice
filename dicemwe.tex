Rolling a 1 on the red die and a 2 on the blue die, denoted \scalebox{0.6}{\maindie{1}{1}\maindie{2}{2}}, is a different outcome than rolling a 2 on the red die and a 1 on the blue die, denoted \scalebox{0.6}{\maindie{2}{1}\maindie{1}{2}}. \\\bigskip

\noindent The sample space for two dice is \\\smallskip

\bigskip

\noindent Dice color can also be inverted (for black and white dice only), using color choice ``3'':\\

\maindie{1}{3}\maindie{6}{3}.

\bigskip

And while, just for example, an 8-sided die has a triangular face and not a rectangular face, it's no effort to make 0, 7, 8, and 9-pip die faces.\\

\maindie{0}{3}\maindie{0}{1}\maindie{7}{1}\maindie{8}{2}\maindie{9}{3}.
